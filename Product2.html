THE SHRIKAR BHAT

Description


An exclusive masterpiece, this royal blue natural dye Patan Patola saree has Shrikar bhat motifs, also known as the 'big elephant' design, depicting the royal hunt - royal hunters astride elephants, peacocks and parrots - framed in typical Ikat checks. The Self blue & black border with Ikat florals is followed with yellow and green horizontal lines. The three part two-side pallu has the black floral border of the saree, a blue band with floral motifs, followed finally by a zari kaddi strip. No blouse attached, so you can wear it with a blouse of your choice. In the Patan Patola saree, the dyeing and the weaving process is extremely complicated, requiring mathematical precision coupled with a vivid imagination. The complexity and intricacy involved in weaving a Patan patola saree involves 4 to 5 weavers working on it over 8 to 9 months. The double Ikat weave means once the saree or fabric is woven, you cannot differentiate between the sides – the colour and the intensity, the feel and the look, are the same on both sides.
